Welcome

Race rules:

  • There will be two races a week, on Wednesday and Saturday during "the season" which runs from the start of October to the end of April.

  • Each race will be repeated three times (to cater for different timezones). Once the route has been published (typically a week in advance) you can sign up for one (or more!) of these on Rouvy as usual. Direct links to the races are also provided directly from our race page which lists dates, times, routes, etc.

  • If none of the official times suit you, you can also arrange your own race. The race must be on the same day (in GMT) as the official races. This gives everybody 24 hours to do the route. Make sure you register your race here so our machinery can extract the finish time and insert it into the compound ranking. The registration page will check that the race is held on the right course and on the right day.

  • You cannot get credit for individual rides (i.e. rides done outside races). This is mainly a technical issue: Rouvy doesn't provide access to the finish times for individual rides and I don't have time to maintain this data by hand. If you absolutely want to ride by yourself, make your own race (see above) and password protect it; keep the password to yourself and nobody will bother you.

  • If you participate in multiple instances of a race, your best time will count.

  • Points will be awarded according to the UCI cyclocross scheme: 40 points for the winner; 30 for second; 25 for third; 20 for fourth; 19 for fifth; then decreasing by one for each subsequent position (but kindly stopping at zero!).

Other rules: Behave yourselves!

There shouldn't be any need for rules. We're all here to have fun racing and that's that.

Sadly, past experience shows that there are certain issues that raise tempers, so to avoid endless (and pointless) discussions, here are a few rules anyway.
  1. Don't be an idiot.

  2. All races must be for "smart trainers only". Rouvy is the sole judge of which trainers fall into that category.

  3. Calibrate your trainer when needed. Most modern trainers don't seem to require this any more anyway but if yours does, do it! Note that it may even make you faster... (I calibrate my Direto X every once in a while and have also done it for the Direto I had before, but it's never made any appreciable difference).

  4. You may have noticed that Rouvy allows you to specify your weight. For the implications of this fact you are referred to rule 1.

  5. Banter on the discussion board is actively encouraged. Taking yourself (or this whole thing) too seriously is not. Launching debates about possible cheaters (or other rule 1 violators) is strictly verboten. If you have any concerns, please contact the race organiser directly and offline, so they can have a quiet word (and/or escalate things if necessary; see below).

  6. No overtly political etc. discussions because it's likely to create tension. This is not censorship - there are plenty of other forums on the internet where you can raise your views on such matters and get into lovely slanging matches with people you disagree with. But not here, please.

  7. Regular rule breakers will be dealt with. Somehow. Haven't decided yet, but punishment could range from flogging (for minor offences) to ejection from the group (for major ones). I suggest we don't go there.

  8. And now go racing! Hard!
